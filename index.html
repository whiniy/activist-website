Fast Fashion

where living in a world filled with overproduced and discarded clothes made with toxic chemicals is the norm

What makes the fashion so...fast?

Fast fashion describes cheap and trendy clothing that were inspired by catwalk pieces and turned into garments for retail stores to meet consumer demand. With new trends constantly becoming popular, these clothes need to be produced quickly and at the least possible cost. As a result, the price of rapid and inexpensive production is a negative impact on the...

Environment

Cheap clothes are made with less environmentally-friendly materials. Toxic dyes, microplastics, and unwanted clothing are released into planet, thus contributing to our never-ending pollution issue.

Employees

To meet consumer demands for trendy items, products must be produced quickly. As a result, almost all factory workers, including children, are severely underpaid, exploited, and work in dangerous conditions.

Society

Fashion advertisements create insecurities, a lack of inclusivity, and much more. For example, many brands such as Shein and FashionNova use unrealistic, photshopped pictures of models in their garments.
